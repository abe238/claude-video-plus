How it works

A question in, an evidence manifest out.

The original samples 100 frames + the full transcript no matter what you asked (~50k tokens on a 40-minute video). Evidence mode compiles what your question actually needs — and writes down why it picked every piece.

$ watch.py "$URL" --detail evidence --question "what's actually new — skip the hype?"

chapter roll-up

Whole topical chapters (YouTube chapters, pause-gap fallback) — not isolated snippets, so features described without your question's words still make it in.

numeric guard

Pricing, benchmarks, and specs get rescued from anywhere in the video — with a frame each, because tables live on-screen, not in speech.

reader rules

The skill instructs the model to mine on-screen tables and reconcile conflicting claims — it caught a presenter's pricing self-contradiction the original repeated.

fail-open

No captions, local file, any compile error → the original pipeline runs instead. You can't end up worse off than upstream.

We publish the round we lost

The first retrieval prototype saved 91% of tokens and lost 3–0 — it missed the pricing segment on a cost question and skipped two headline features. That failing round ships in the repo next to the wins, because it's what the shipped mechanisms actually fix. If a future benchmark shows a loss, it ships too.

Tradeoffs

What you give up. Directly.

  • Needs a question. No --question, no evidence mode — you get the original pipeline, unchanged.
  • Slightly slower end-to-end. ~2s of compile after download, plus more careful frame reading. Tokens down a lot, wall-clock up a little.
  • URLs with captions only, today. Local files and caption-less videos fall back to the original automatically.
  • Summaries keep the full transcript by design — top-k retrieval on a summary is how you miss stories. The −60% there comes from smarter frames + dedup.
  • Retrieval is lexical, not semantic. Zero word-overlap questions can under-retrieve; the guard, facet expansion, and fallback blunt this.
  • The benchmark is honest but small. LLM judges, paired blind panels, auditable raw data — not yet the preregistered statistical trial specified in the repo's improvement plan.
